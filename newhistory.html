Executive Summary: Margin Expansion Through Scale

New History's financial model demonstrates a clear path from early-stage startup to profitable operations through systematic volume growth and manufacturing efficiency. The business achieves profitability in Month 9 and shows dramatic margin improvement as production scales.

üéØ The Critical Insight: This model demonstrates that beverage startups don't achieve profitability through price increases‚Äîthey achieve it through volume-driven COGS reduction. Every volume milestone unlocks better per-unit economics, expanding margins from 48.8% to 65.2% while maintaining consistent pricing.

Why These Numbers Matter: The journey from startup production (1,200 units/month in Month 1) to scaled production (147,201 units/month by Year 3 end) isn't just about revenue growth. It fundamentally transforms the business economics. Unit COGS decreases 28% from $10.95 to $7.90, which on a $27.54 wholesale price converts a decent-margin business into a highly profitable one.

Year 1 Strategy: Year 1 focuses on proving product-market fit and building velocity. With $636K in cash requirements (averaging ~$53K/month), the business achieves operating profitability in Month 9. The year closes with 2,500 retail accounts and 4.62 cases/month average velocity‚Äîsolid metrics for Series A fundraising.

The Growth Trajectory: Year 2 transforms the business with 12% monthly customer growth and manufacturing efficiency gains. COGS per unit drops to $9.12 (17% reduction), pushing gross margins to 59.5%. The business generates $2.9M in operating income, demonstrating scalable profitability. Year 3 continues this momentum with further COGS optimization to $7.90/unit and margins expanding to 65.2%.
